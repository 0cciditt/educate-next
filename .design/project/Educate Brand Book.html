North Star

A brand that teaches with both hands.

02 / 19

One hand is trustworthy — the steady, organized hand parents shake before signing their kid up. The other is joyful — the hand a 7-year-old grabs on the way into class.

Every page, button, color and word should hold both at once. Skew too corporate and parents trust you but kids tune out. Skew too cartoonish and kids love you but parents won't pay tuition. The system is built to live in the middle, on purpose.

Confident

Clear hierarchy. Strong geometric type. Real photography.

Warm

Rounded shapes. Generous air. Friendly voice in second person.

Playful

Color-coded levels. Building-block motif. Joyful illustration accents.

Grounded

Quiet neutrals. Honest, specific copy. No empty marketing.

Voice — say / don't say
✓
"Your child will read their first English chapter book by June."

Concrete. A specific outcome a parent can picture.

×
"World-class English language acquisition methodology."

Corporate. Nothing a 9-year-old or their mom can grab onto.

RULES OF THUMB
  • Speak directly to a parent or a child — never to "users."
  • Lead with verbs: learn, build, read, speak, play.
  • One exclamation per page, maximum.
  • Numbers and outcomes beat adjectives every time.

Methodology · How we teach

We measure progress, not effort.

Every twelve weeks, your child sits with a teacher and a parent for a fifteen-minute review. We open the same assessment from week one and read it together.

Nothing is hidden, nothing is fluffed. If a unit didn't land, we say so and rework the plan. If it did, you'll watch your kid explain — in English — what they learned and what they want to learn next.

That's it. That's the whole method. Read the full curriculum →

PHOTOGRAPHY · CANDID · WARM

Real students, real teachers, real classrooms. Natural light, tight crops on faces and hands. No flat-lay laptops, no stock-corporate handshakes.

DO

Candid moments. Warm white balance. Eyes engaged. Mixed ages and backgrounds.

DON'T

Stock posed shots. Black & white. Heavy filters. Anyone facing camera with a thumbs up.
